<p><p>The hollow fiber membrane market comprises three primary types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration, with larger pore sizes, effectively removes particles, bacteria, and sediments, making it suitable for water treatment and food processing. Ultrafiltration, with smaller pores, targets viruses and colloids, widely used in wastewater treatment and beverage production. Nanofiltration operates at even finer levels, allowing selective ion removal and softening, finding applications in water softening and agrochemical separation. Each type serves distinct filtration needs across various industries.</p></p>
