<p><p>Sulfide-based solid-state batteries primarily fall into two categories: all-solid-state batteries and semi-solid batteries. All-solid-state batteries utilize solid electrolytes entirely, offering enhanced safety and energy density. They are advantageous for high-performance applications but face challenges in manufacturing scalability and material stability. In contrast, semi-solid batteries incorporate a mixture of solid and liquid electrolytes, striking a balance between performance and manufacturability. These hybrid designs aim to improve energy storage efficiency while simplifying production processes, catering to evolving energy storage needs.</p></p>
